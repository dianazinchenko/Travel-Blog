 London is the capital city of England and the United Kingdom, and is one of the largest cities in the world. With a rich history and cultural heritage. It is a hub of activity, attracting millions of visitors each year. From its iconic landmarks such as Big Ben, Buckingham Palace and the Tower of London, to its world-class museums and art galleries, there is something for everyone to enjoy.
As a kid I always wanted to live in England, especially London. Although I wouldn't call it my favourite city, it still has a special place in my heart. There is something about the bustling streets of London and its aristocratic association that makes me love it. On top of that, it's also a huge city. There is so much to do and occupy yourself with, such as trying new restaurants, going to a market, visiting a museum, watching a musical, strolling around Hyde Park or Regent's park (both are my favourite), going to a new coffee shop, among others.
No matter how many times I come back to London, I am always excited to be there.

Portugal is a beautiful country located on the western coast of the Iberian Peninsula, known for its stunning coastline, historic cities, and rich culture.
I consider Portugal my home town, since I've lived here for almost my whole life. I've travelled all over the country, but my favourite city will always be Porto. It has everything that a person would want - great history, amazing architecture, beautiful beaches and fantastic food. I decided to include Portugal in the blog because I really believe it's a country that has to be visited - its culture will make you fall in love with it.

Favourite city in Spain, really loved everything about it

Spain is a diverse country with a rich culture, fascinating history, and beautiful landscapes. The country is also famous for its delicious cuisine, including tapas, paella, and sangria, as well as its flamenco dance and bullfighting traditions. Whether you're looking to relax on the beach, experience the local culture, or explore the countryside, it is a must-visit destination for travelers.
Spain has always been the top destination of my childhood Summer holidays. The all-inclusive Summers will always be a highlight in my memory with all its desserts, slushies and hotel entertainment.
My most recent visit to Spain was right before my birthday, when I visited Madrid. I really didn't think that I'd like it that much, but I really did. Its streets were very clean with a lot of greenery everywhere. Overall, I really enjoyed my time there, just walking and exploring the city.

Hungary is a country located in central Europe and is known for its rich history, thermal baths, and diverse landscapes. The capital city of Budapest is a highlight, with its stunning architecture, thermal baths, and rich cultural heritage.
I visited Budapest during Christmas time, which made the most sense. It's a great city with a lot of character. I would like to point out that it's probably the place with the most unique Christmas markets - the light shows were very impressive. One of my favourite activities there was the Thermal Baths. The building itself was very beautiful and it was such a nice feeling getting into the hot water after being in the freezing cold.

France is a country renowned for its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and iconic landmarks. From the bustling city of Paris, with its iconic Eiffel Tower and world-famous museums, to the rolling vineyards of the countryside, France has something to offer every type of traveler.
The first time I visited Paris it felt like I didn't have enough time to see it all. I enjoyed the sunny days exploring the Parisian streets and all the pastries and baguettes and cakes that I ate on the way, obviously. When I was in Paris, I also did a daytrip to Versailles to the beautiful castle and walk around its vast and charming gardens.
Both times that I visited Paris I went to Disneyland, which was the best part. I love how happy everyone is there and it's like being in a different place, disconnected from the real world.

Poland is a country located in the center of Eastern Europe and recently I got to travel to its capital. The timing of my trip to Warsaw wasn't how I planned, because most of the Christmas lights weren't working yet nor were most of the decorations up yet. Nonetheless, I still loved the city. It felt like New York but in eastern Europe and the view from the Airbnb was truly amazing. Even though the Christmas lights hadn't been turned on at the time, I still got to enjoy one of the markets in the Old Town. Overall, I really enjoyed the city and it's cuisine. I got to try mash and cottage cheese pierogies, but my favourite food were the donuts - they were truly one of a kind.
